Intelligent Quantum Materials Lab (IQM Lab)

We study strongly correlated electron materials using first-principles calculations, model simulations, and many-body numerical methods, with a focus on superconductivity, magnetism, ferroelectricity, and multiferroicity in transition-metal compounds.

Contact the PI Google Scholar

About the PI

Ling-Fang Lin is a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Tennessee, working with Prof. Elbio Dagotto and Prof. Adriana Moreo. She received her Ph.D. in Condensed Matter Physics from Southeast University.

CV snapshot: Google Scholar citations about 2500 and h-index 25, as of September 2025. Please verify before public release.

Research Directions

Nickelate and cuprate superconductors
Electronic structure, pairing tendencies, and magnetic orders in high-temperature superconducting materials.
Magnetism and multiferroicity
Transition-metal oxides and fluorides, exchange mechanisms, orbital ordering, and magnetoelectric effects.
Computational methods
VASP, Wannier90, model Hamiltonians, DMRG++, Lanczos methods, and related numerical workflows.
